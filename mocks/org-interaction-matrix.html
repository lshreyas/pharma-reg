The org, mapped onto the documents

A drug dossier is produced by a cross-functional assembly line, not a writing team. Each row is a functional group; each column is a document. A marked cell shows that group's role: author (owns the draft), contributor (supplies data or sections), reviewer, or approver (signs off). Click a group to see its footprint, or a document to see who assembles it.

Roles are representative of a typical mid-to-large biopharma operating model; titles and boundaries vary by company (some merge Regulatory Ops into Regulatory Affairs, or Drug Safety into a broader PV org). "Author" often means "orchestrates + writes," with Medical Writing doing the actual drafting from others' inputs. The point is the interaction pattern, not any one org chart.
